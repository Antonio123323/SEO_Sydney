These scams usually follow a predictable pattern. A new “coin” is launched with a professional-looking website and whitepaper. Influencers or fake accounts on social media promote it as the next big thing. They claim that well-known business leaders or even the government support it. Once people start buying, the price rises sharply — and then the developers disappear, leaving investors with losses.

Here’s how such crypto frauds typically operate:

  • Step 1: Launch a token with a famous-sounding name (like Tata, Ambani, or Modi).

  • Step 3: Attract quick investment from the public.
  • Step 4: Manipulate the tata cryptocurrency price or similar coins to create artificial demand.
