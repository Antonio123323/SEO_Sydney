Regulations in India: Compliance and Taxes

Cryptocurrency regulation in India is evolving quickly. While the government has not yet declared crypto as legal tender, it allows trading under specific guidelines. Projects like Big Bull Cryptocurrency must follow these frameworks to ensure transparency and investor safety.

At present, Indian crypto exchanges operate under strict KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) rules. Every user must verify their identity before buying or selling digital assets. This protects against fraud and illegal transactions, helping the market gain credibility.

In terms of taxation, India introduced a 30% tax on profits earned from cryptocurrency trading, along with a 1% TDS (tax deducted at source) on each trade. For many retail investors, this created short-term challenges but also increased the legitimacy of crypto as an investment category. Understanding these tax rules is essential for anyone planning to trade or hold tokens like Big Bull.
