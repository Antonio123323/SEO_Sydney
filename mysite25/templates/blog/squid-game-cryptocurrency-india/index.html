Warning Signs of a Crypto Scam

The Squid Game cryptocurrency case is now studied as one of the most famous examples of a “rug pull” scam. Understanding how it happened helps investors recognize red flags in other coins. Many people in India still ask how to tell if a token like kibo cryptocurrency fake or real is safe — the truth is, scams often look professional on the surface but hide danger underneath.

Main Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Anonymous Developers: Legitimate projects usually reveal their founders and technical team. Squid Game’s team used fake identities and deleted profiles after the launch.
  • No Whitepaper or Roadmap: Real tokens explain how the project works and what problems it solves. The Squid Game token had only a short web page with vague promises.
  • Locked or Restricted Sales: Investors discovered they could buy the coin but couldn’t sell it. This is a classic scam feature hidden in the contract code.
  • No Audit or Transparency: Every trustworthy token is verified by an independent smart contract auditor. Squid Game had none.
  • Unrealistic Price Growth: A 1000x increase in days might sound exciting, but it’s also a major warning sign. Real projects grow steadily, not explosively.
