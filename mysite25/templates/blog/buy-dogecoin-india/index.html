Legal Status of Dogecoin in India

Before you buy Dogecoin cryptocurrency, it’s important to know its legal status in India. All profits from crypto trading are taxed at a flat rate of 30%. In addition, a 1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is applied to each transaction. This law applies whether you trade Dogecoin or any other crypto, such as Shiba Inu.

Storing Dogecoin Safely

Once you buy Dogecoin cryptocurrency, your next priority should be keeping it safe. Digital coins like Dogecoin are stored in wallets — secure apps or devices that hold your private keys. Losing these keys means losing access to your crypto forever. That’s why proper storage is essential for every investor in India.

Types of Dogecoin Wallets

There are two main types of wallets for Dogecoin:

  • Hot Wallets – Online wallets connected to the internet. They are convenient for quick access and trading. Examples include Trust Wallet and MetaMask.
  • Cold Wallets – Offline storage devices that keep your Dogecoin safe from hackers. Hardware wallets such as Ledger and Trezor are popular options.

If you plan to trade frequently, hot wallets work well because they’re fast and simple. But if you want to hold Dogecoin long-term, cold wallets are safer because they aren’t exposed to online threats.

Security Tips for Indian Investors

To protect your Dogecoin, follow these simple safety measures:

  • Always enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your exchange and wallet.
  • Keep your seed phrase (a recovery code of 12–24 words) written down on paper, not stored online.
  • Never share your private keys or passwords with anyone.
  • Check website URLs carefully before logging in — fake crypto sites can steal your data.
  • Regularly update your wallet app and enable security patches.

Many Indian traders make the mistake of leaving their Dogecoin on exchanges. While exchanges are convenient, they are also frequent targets of hacking. For long-term investors, transferring Dogecoin to a private wallet offers stronger protection.

Taxation and Regulations for Dogecoin in India

Every Indian investor who decides to buy Dogecoin cryptocurrency must also understand the tax laws that apply to digital assets. The government of India treats all cryptocurrencies, including Dogecoin and Shiba Inu, as virtual digital assets (VDAs). That means any profit made from buying, selling, or trading these coins is taxable.

Current Tax Rules

Under the Indian Income Tax Act, profits earned from crypto trading are subject to a 30% flat tax rate. This applies whether you make gains from selling Dogecoin or exchanging it for another token. Additionally, every crypto transaction is charged a 1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source), which the exchange automatically deducts when you sell or swap your coins.

Here’s an example for better understanding:

  • If you bought Dogecoin worth ₹10,000 and later sold it for ₹15,000, your profit is ₹5,000.
  • That ₹5,000 profit is taxable at 30%, meaning you’ll pay ₹1,500 in tax.
  • The exchange will also deduct 1% TDS during the transaction.

How to Stay Compliant

To stay compliant with Indian law, you should:

  • Maintain detailed records of all your crypto transactions — purchase date, amount, and cryptocurrency Dogecoin price in India at the time of purchase.
  • Report your gains while filing your annual tax returns.
  • Use exchanges and platforms that automatically calculate TDS and provide downloadable transaction histories.
