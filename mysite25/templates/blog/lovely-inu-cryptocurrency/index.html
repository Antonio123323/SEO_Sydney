Legal Status of Cryptocurrency in India

As of 2025, India has not introduced a specific law to regulate cryptocurrencies, but several financial guidelines apply. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) monitors exchanges through strict KYC and anti-money laundering policies. This means users must trade through verified platforms such as BitTrade AI, which ensures compliance and user safety.

While crypto is legal to own and trade, it is not recognized as legal tender. You can buy, sell, and hold coins like Lovely cryptocurrency, but you cannot use them to pay for goods or services within India’s traditional financial system.

Taxation on Cryptocurrency Gains

In India, profits from cryptocurrencies are taxed at a flat rate of 30% under the Income Tax Act, regardless of the investor’s income bracket. Additionally, a 1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) applies to every crypto transaction above a certain threshold. This applies even to small penny cryptocurrency trades.

Trading (Buy/Sell) 30% on gains Calculated after deducting purchase cost
