Legal Status and Regulations in India

India’s relationship with cryptocurrency has always been complex. While digital assets like Bitcoin and Ethereum are not banned, the government maintains strict oversight of all crypto-related activities. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and the Ministry of Finance have made it clear that cryptocurrencies are not legal tender, meaning they cannot be used as official currency for payments.

For investors asking whether Kibo cryptocurrency is fake or real, this legal context is very important. In India, crypto assets are allowed to exist, but trading or promoting unverified tokens can fall into a grey zone. This means that platforms like Kibho, which promise fixed returns and recruit users through MLM-style schemes, could be operating outside the limits of financial regulations.

1. Taxation and Reporting

The Indian government introduced a 30% tax on profits from cryptocurrency trading and a 1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) on transactions. These laws apply only to recognized exchanges and registered coins. Since Kibo or Kibho are not listed on verified exchanges such as WazirX or CoinDCX, it is unclear how — or if — these taxes apply. This makes the investment legally uncertain and risky.

2. Lack of Registration

So far, there is no official record of Kibo or Kibho being registered under India’s Companies Act or listed as a recognized crypto project. The absence of such registration increases the risk that the token might vanish overnight, leaving investors with no legal protection or refund options.
