What Is Pi Cryptocurrency and How It Works

Pi cryptocurrency is a digital currency created to make crypto participation easy for everyone. Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum, you do not need expensive hardware or high electricity costs to mine Pi. The mining process happens through the Pi Network mobile app, which is available for Android and iOS users.

The project was developed by a team of Stanford graduates led by Dr. Nicolas Kokkalis. Their mission was to build a cryptocurrency that is inclusive and sustainable. Users can earn Pi coins by simply opening the app once a day and pressing a lightning button. The app uses a consensus algorithm called the Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP), which allows transactions to be verified through trusted nodes instead of energy-intensive mining.

Another key feature of Pi Network is its strong community. Each user contributes to the network’s growth by inviting others and forming a “security circle.” These circles increase trust and decentralization. This social structure is one of the reasons Pi has attracted millions of users globally, especially in India, where community-driven technologies grow fast.

Currently, the network is in its enclosed mainnet phase. This means users can transfer Pi within the app ecosystem but not yet on public exchanges. Once the network transitions to the open mainnet, Pi is expected to be listed on global exchanges, which could officially establish the pi cryptocurrency price in INR and other fiat currencies.

Until then, the value of Pi cryptocurrency in INR seen on price-tracking platforms like CoinGecko or Bitget is based on community estimation. Still, this early stage gives users in India an opportunity to join the ecosystem before the coin reaches full market circulation.
