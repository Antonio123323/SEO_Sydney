How to Store

After purchasing, storing your cryptocurrency safely is the next step. You have two main options:

  • Exchange Wallets: Convenient but risky if the exchange is hacked. Use this only for active trading.
  • Non-Custodial Wallets: Such as hardware or software wallets where you control private keys. This is safer for long-term holding.

When using a non-custodial wallet, always back up your seed phrase and never share it. Store it offline in a secure place, not in your phone or email. This is the only way to recover your funds if the device is lost.

For additional safety, enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on every account and use unique, strong passwords. Avoid using public Wi-Fi when accessing wallets or exchanges.

Legal and Tax Aspects for Indian Investors

The legal status of cryptocurrencies in India has been evolving. While digital assets like the Salman Khan Cryptocurrency are not banned, they are not officially recognized as legal tender either. This means investors can buy, sell, and trade them, but must follow certain tax and compliance rules set by Indian authorities.

Regulatory Landscape

India’s government and the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) have taken a cautious approach toward cryptocurrencies. They’ve warned investors about potential risks such as market volatility and fraud. However, they have not outlawed trading or holding crypto assets. The introduction of a 30% tax on crypto profits in 2022 confirmed that the government acknowledges crypto as a taxable asset class, not an illegal one.

Taxation Rules

  • Income Tax: Profits from the sale or exchange of digital assets like Salman Khan Cryptocurrency, big bull cryptocurrency, or kibho cryptocurrency are taxed at a flat rate of 30%, without any deductions for expenses.
  • TDS (Tax Deducted at Source): A 1% TDS is applied on every crypto transaction above a specific threshold.
  • Losses: Losses from one crypto trade cannot be offset against gains from another, as per the current law.
  • Record Keeping: Investors must maintain detailed records of all transactions, including date, value, exchange used, and wallet address.

Failing to declare crypto income can lead to penalties under Indian tax law. Therefore, it’s essential to report crypto holdings transparently during annual income tax filings.

Compliance and Security

Always use exchanges that comply with Indian KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) regulations. This not only ensures legal safety but also protects your funds against potential scams. Avoid peer-to-peer trades with unknown parties and verify every transaction source.
