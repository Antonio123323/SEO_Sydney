Regulations and Crypto Laws in Singapore

Singapore is known for its strong financial system. It applies the same care to cryptocurrencies. If you want to buy crypto in Singapore, it’s important to follow local rules.

The main regulator is the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). It makes sure that all crypto businesses follow the rules. Any exchange or platform offering crypto must apply for a special license called the Digital Payment Token (DPT) license. Without it, they cannot legally serve users in Singapore.

In 2024, MAS introduced even stricter rules. These changes will take full effect on June 30, 2025. The goal is to protect users and reduce the risk of fraud or loss. Key parts of the new law include:

  • No credit cards: Crypto platforms will not be allowed to accept credit card payments issued in Singapore.
  • No ads that promise big profits: Marketing must be clear and honest. Platforms cannot suggest guaranteed returns.
  • Platforms must hold customer funds separately: This helps prevent misuse of your money if a company fails.

If a company breaks these rules, it can face fines of up to SGD 250,000 and jail time of up to three years. So, it’s very important to choose a platform that is licensed by MAS.

As a user, this is good news. You can now avoid risky websites and focus only on safe, approved services. This also means you won’t need to worry about your funds being frozen or lost due to fraud.

Some people use P2P platforms to buy crypto directly from other users. However, this method comes with high risks, such as scams and fake profiles.

Since 2026 regulations are stricter, P2P trading is not recommended unless you’re experienced and know the risks.

How to Store Your Cryptocurrency Safely

Once you buy cryptocurrency, you need a safe place to store it. Leaving your coins on an exchange is convenient, but not always the safest option. If the exchange gets hacked or shut down, you could lose access to your funds.

There are two main ways to store crypto:

1. Hot Wallets

Hot wallets are connected to the internet. These include apps or web wallets provided by exchanges like Coinbase or Crypto.com. They’re easy to use and good for beginners or small amounts.

  • Fast access to your crypto
  • Useful for daily trading
  • Less secure if not protected properly

Example: The Gemini app includes a hot wallet with insurance coverage.

2. Cold Wallets

Cold wallets are offline devices. They are not connected to the internet, so they are much safer from hacks and malware. These wallets are best for long-term storage.

Types of cold wallets:

  • Hardware wallets: Devices like Ledger and Trezor keep your private keys offline.
  • Paper wallets: Printed QR codes or seed phrases stored in a secure place.

If you're investing large amounts or holding crypto for the long term, a hardware wallet is highly recommended. Some investors store their coins in a cold wallet and only move small amounts to a hot wallet when needed.

Security Tips

  • Use strong passwords and enable 2FA (two-factor authentication).
  • Back up your wallet’s seed phrase and keep it offline.
  • Don’t share your private keys with anyone.

Storing your crypto safely is just as important as buying it. Think of your wallet as a digital bank vault. The more steps you take to protect it, the safer your assets will be.

Crypto Taxes and Legal Aspects

One of the big advantages of buying crypto in Singapore is the clear tax system. The government has created rules that are simple to understand, especially for individual investors.

1. No Capital Gains Tax

In Singapore, you don’t pay tax on capital gains. This means that if you buy Bitcoin and later sell it at a profit, you usually won’t owe any tax. This applies to most personal investments.

However, if you are actively trading as a business or earning regular income from crypto, you may be taxed as a business activity.

2. Goods and Services Tax (GST)

Crypto that is defined as a Digital Payment Token (DPT) is exempt from GST. Most popular coins like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and USDC fall under this category.

But if you deal with crypto that is not classified as DPT, GST may apply. Always check the status of the token with the platform or consult a tax expert.

3. Reporting Requirements

Singapore doesn’t require individuals to declare crypto holdings unless they are part of a business. Still, it’s a good idea to keep records of your:

  • Purchase dates and prices
  • Selling dates and values
  • Wallet addresses and transactions

This can help if you ever need to prove your transactions or move assets internationally.

Legal Use of Crypto

You are free to buy, hold, and sell crypto in Singapore using MAS-approved platforms. However, using crypto for illegal purposes like money laundering is strictly forbidden and punishable under Singapore law.

Tip: Always use licensed platforms and keep your personal documents updated to meet KYC (Know Your Customer) rules.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even in a safe environment like Singapore, crypto users can still make costly mistakes. Avoiding these will help protect your money and your identity.

1. Using Unlicensed Platforms

Some websites offer high returns or low fees but are not regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). These platforms can shut down without warning or steal your funds. Always check that the platform is MAS-licensed before you invest.

2. Paying with Credit Cards

Using credit cards for crypto purchases may soon be banned in Singapore. Even now, it’s not recommended. You could face high fees and interest charges. Debit cards or FAST bank transfers are better options.

3. Leaving Crypto on Exchanges

After buying crypto, many people leave it on the exchange. If the exchange is hacked, your coins might be lost. For better security, transfer your crypto to a personal wallet, especially for long-term storage.

4. Forgetting to Back Up Wallets

If you use a hardware or software wallet, always save your seed phrase. Without it, you can’t recover your crypto if your device is lost or broken.

By avoiding these mistakes, you reduce your risk and build a safer path in crypto investing.
