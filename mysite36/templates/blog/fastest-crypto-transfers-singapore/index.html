Evaluation Criteria for Fast Crypto Transfers

Not all crypto apps are built for speed. To choose the right one, it’s important to understand what makes a platform fast and reliable — especially in Singapore’s financial environment. Below are the key factors we used to evaluate the best apps for quick crypto transfers.

1. Support for FAST and PayNow

FAST (Fast and Secure Transfers) and PayNow are local payment rails in Singapore. They allow almost instant SGD transfers between banks and licensed platforms. A crypto app that supports these systems lets you move money in and out of your account quickly. This is essential for buying or selling crypto when timing matters.

2. Local Bank Integration

Apps with connections to major Singaporean banks (like DBS, OCBC, and UOB) can process deposits and withdrawals faster. Some platforms partner directly with banks to offer near-instant processing of fiat transactions, which is crucial for traders and investors.

3. Instant Crypto Wallet Transfers

Internal transfers — such as sending funds between users on the same app — should be near-instant. Platforms that offer fast peer-to-peer (P2P) transfers allow you to move crypto between wallets or users without waiting for blockchain confirmations.

4. Processing Time for Deposits and Withdrawals

Speed depends not only on the system used, but also on how quickly the app processes your request. Some platforms batch withdrawals or only process them during office hours. The best apps handle deposit and withdrawal requests automatically, 24/7.

5. Regulation and Licensing

Speed should never come at the cost of safety. In Singapore, crypto platforms are regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Apps with a MAS license must follow strict rules for financial operations, security, and user protection. These platforms are also more likely to offer stable and trustworthy services.

6. Easy User Interface (UX)

Fast transfers depend on how quickly a user can act. Platforms with a simple, intuitive interface make it easier to send, receive, and convert crypto. Mobile-first design, clear menus, and low friction for repeat actions help speed things up for everyone — from beginners to seasoned traders.

  • Example: Some apps allow you to set favorite wallets or preset transfer amounts for one-tap sending.
  • Example: Others give live status updates for each transaction, which builds user trust.

Security, Regulation, and Compliance

Fast transfers are important, but security and legal compliance should never be ignored. In Singapore, only platforms regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) can offer crypto services legally. Choosing a licensed platform helps protect your funds and personal information.

Why Regulation Matters

MAS ensures that crypto platforms follow proper procedures. This includes safeguarding client funds, verifying user identity (KYC), and tracking suspicious activity (AML rules). Platforms that meet these standards are less likely to be involved in fraud, downtime, or sudden closures.

For example: a licensed exchange must hold your fiat funds in separate trust accounts. This means your money isn't mixed with the company's own operations — and stays safe even if the business faces financial trouble.

Compliance Requirements

  • Travel Rule: Platforms must share sender and receiver info for transfers between exchanges. This adds a layer of accountability.
  • KYC (Know Your Customer): Users must verify identity to use core features like withdrawals or fiat funding.
  • AML (Anti-Money Laundering): All transactions are monitored to detect unusual behavior, such as rapid high-volume transfers.

Unregulated Platforms: A Risk

There are many apps that promise instant crypto profits or zero fees. But if they aren’t licensed, you risk losing your money. Always check if a platform is on the MAS list of approved providers before signing up. Fast doesn’t mean safe — unless backed by regulation.

In our top picks, OKX, Coinhako, Gemini, and Crypto.com are all MAS-licensed. Coinbase is still in the licensing process but operates legally under an exemption.
