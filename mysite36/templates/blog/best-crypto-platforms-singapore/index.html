Regulation and Security in Singapore

One of the main reasons people trust crypto platforms in Singapore is strong regulation. The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) controls how digital asset services work. It sets rules that make sure users are protected, and that companies operate fairly and transparently.

What Is MAS and Why It Matters

MAS is Singapore’s central bank and financial regulator. It issues licenses to companies that want to offer digital payment token (DPT) services. Only platforms that meet strict rules on safety, anti-money laundering, and customer verification can get a license.

Some of the biggest global names like Coinbase, Crypto.com, Gemini, and OKX are licensed or officially approved to operate in Singapore. Local platforms such as Coinhako and Independent Reserve also hold licenses. This shows they meet the country’s high regulatory standards.

Why You Should Use a Licensed Platform

Choosing a licensed crypto platform gives you better security. These platforms must follow strong rules:

  • KYC (Know Your Customer): You must verify your identity before trading.
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML): Transactions are monitored to stop illegal use.
  • Customer Fund Protection: Assets are often held in cold storage and insured.
  • Local Support: SGD deposits and withdrawals are supported through trusted channels like FAST and PayNow.

Unlicensed or offshore platforms may offer high returns or low fees, but they come with big risks. In fact, Binance.sg exited the Singapore market in 2022 due to regulatory challenges. This highlights why using regulated platforms is not just safer — it’s necessary.

MAS Watchlist and Updates

MAS also keeps a public list of companies under review or warning. Before signing up for a platform, it’s smart to check the Investor Alert List on the MAS website.

As of 2026, MAS is also looking into more advanced topics like automated crypto platforms and DeFi services. This shows that Singapore is not only secure, but also future-ready when it comes to innovation in crypto trading and investing.

Key Factors to Consider When Choosing a Crypto Platform

Choosing the right crypto platform is not just about picking the most popular name. It’s about finding the service that fits your needs, risk level, and financial goals. Here are the most important factors to look at when selecting a cryptocurrency platform in Singapore.

1. MAS Licensing

Always check if the platform is licensed or approved by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). A regulated platform gives you more protection, clearer tax rules, and better local support.

2. SGD Support and Payment Methods

Look for platforms that support direct SGD transactions. This allows you to use methods like:

  • FAST: Instant local bank transfers
  • PayNow: Quick payments using mobile numbers
  • Xfers or StraitsX: Payment gateways used in local crypto apps

Platforms that support SGD reduce the need for currency conversion and extra fees.

3. Trading and Withdrawal Fees

Fees can eat into your profits. Always check for:

  • Trading fees: Charged per buy/sell transaction (e.g., 0.4%–1.5%)
  • Deposit/withdrawal fees: Some platforms offer free SGD deposits, others charge up to 1.0%
  • Hidden costs: Such as spread between buy and sell prices

4. Platform Security

Your crypto should be safe. Key security features include:

  • Two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Cold storage: Funds stored offline
  • Insurance: Protection against hacks or platform failure
  • Withdrawal whitelists: Allow transfers only to pre-approved wallets

5. Ease of Use

Some platforms are made for beginners, with simple dashboards and mobile apps. Others offer advanced tools for traders, like charts, API access, or bots for automated crypto trading. Choose based on your skill level.

6. Extra Features

If you’re interested in more than just buying and selling crypto, look for platforms that offer:

  • Staking: Earn passive income by locking crypto
  • NFT trading: Buy and sell digital collectibles
  • Access to Web3: Wallet integration and dApps

Think about what you need now and what you may want in the future. For example, a beginner might want a clean interface, while an active investor may look for low trading fees and DeFi options.

How to Choose the Right Platform for You

With so many crypto platforms available in Singapore, making a choice can feel overwhelming. But it doesn’t have to be. Start by asking yourself a few simple questions about your goals, habits, and experience.

Step 1: Define Your Purpose

What do you want to do with crypto?

  • Buy and hold? Look for platforms with safe wallets and easy fiat transfers like Coinhako or Gemini.
  • Trade actively? Choose low-fee platforms with fast execution like OKX or Crypto.com.
  • Earn passive income? Use platforms that support staking or yield products like Gemini Earn or JuCoin.
  • Explore Web3? You’ll want wallet access and dApp integration like on OKX or JuCoin.

Step 2: Consider Your Payment Options

Do you want to use Singapore Dollars (SGD) directly? Choose platforms that support FAST, PayNow, or Xfers payments. This makes deposits fast, cheap, and reliable. Coinhako, Independent Reserve, and Crypto.com all support SGD.

Step 3: Check the Fees

Don’t just look at trading fees. Watch out for:

  • Deposit/withdrawal costs
  • Spreads between buying and selling prices
  • Hidden fees on token swaps or transfers

If you plan to trade often, even a 0.1% difference can matter a lot over time.

Step 4: Think About Security

Security should never be ignored. Choose a platform that offers:

  • Two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Cold storage for most funds
  • Licensing from MAS
  • Insurance policies on digital assets

Final Tip:

Start with one or two platforms. Test their apps, explore features, and see what works best for you. Many users use one platform for trading and another for long-term investing. That’s completely fine.
