Types of Crypto Wallets

Not all crypto wallets are the same. Before choosing one, it’s helpful to understand how they work and what makes each type different. In Singapore, people use a mix of online and offline wallets — depending on how often they trade or how much they store.

1. Hot Wallets (Online Wallets)

Hot wallets work through the internet. You install them on your phone or browser. They are fast and simple, great for buying and sending crypto every day. But because they’re connected online, they can be more open to hacking.

  • Examples: Best Wallet, MetaMask
  • Good for: Fast access and trading
  • Watch out for: Online risks if not secured properly

2. Cold Wallets (Offline Wallets)

Cold wallets are physical devices or cards that stay offline. They store your private keys without needing an internet connection. This makes them very safe — even if your phone or laptop is compromised.

  • Examples: Tangem, Ledger Nano, Bitkey
  • Good for: Long-term storage and big savings
  • Downside: Slower to use for daily transfers

3. Custodial Wallets

These wallets are managed by a company, like a crypto exchange. The service keeps your keys and handles security. It’s easier for beginners, but also means you trust someone else with your funds.

  • Examples: Crypto.com, Coinhako
  • Good for: New users who want simple setup
  • Downside: You don’t control the private keys

4. Non-Custodial Wallets

Non-custodial wallets give you full control. You hold your keys, no third party involved. Many crypto users in Singapore prefer this option for privacy and independence.

  • Examples: Best Wallet, Tangem, MetaMask
  • Good for: Users who want more control and safety
  • Important: You must back up your recovery phrase

Tip: A smart strategy is to use both types — keep small amounts in a hot wallet for quick use, and store larger amounts in a cold wallet for safety.

Crypto Rules and Security in Singapore

Singapore is known for having clear rules when it comes to digital money. The government takes crypto seriously and works to protect users. That’s why many people feel safe buying and holding crypto here.

Who Regulates Crypto?

The main regulator is MAS — the Monetary Authority of Singapore. They control how crypto companies operate. Under the Payment Services Act, companies must register and follow rules to offer crypto services. This includes exchanges, wallets, and payment apps.

If a crypto platform is licensed by MAS, it means they follow strict standards — like keeping your money safe, preventing fraud, and verifying user identities. Using licensed platforms adds trust and reduces risk.

KYC: Know Your Customer

In Singapore, most wallets and platforms ask for ID checks. This is called KYC — short for “Know Your Customer.” You may need to upload a photo ID or verify with Singpass. While it takes a few minutes, KYC helps keep scammers out and keeps your account safe.

Security Features to Look For

Whether you're using a mobile app or a hardware wallet, make sure it has good protection. Look for:

  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): A second login step, like a code sent to your phone.
  • Biometric login: Use your face or fingerprint to open the app.
  • Backup seed phrase: A secret code to recover your wallet if you lose your device.
  • Multi-signature: For extra safety, some wallets require more than one device to approve a transaction.

How to Stay Safe

Even in a secure place like Singapore, scams can happen. Never click on random links or download wallets from unofficial sources. Always double-check website addresses and make sure you're using the official app store.
