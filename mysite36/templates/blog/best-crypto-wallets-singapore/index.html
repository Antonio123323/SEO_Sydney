Types of Crypto Wallets

Before choosing the best crypto wallet in Singapore, it’s important to understand the main types. Each wallet type works differently and offers different levels of control and protection. Some are best for fast access. Others are built for long-term security.

1. Hot Wallets

Hot wallets are connected to the internet. They are usually apps or browser extensions. They are fast, easy to use, and great for daily transactions. However, they can be more vulnerable to online attacks.

  • Examples: MetaMask, Best Wallet
  • Pros: Quick access, easy to install, support for DeFi and NFTs
  • Cons: Less secure than cold wallets

2. Cold Wallets

Cold wallets are offline devices. They store your private keys without an internet connection. This makes them very secure, even if your computer or phone is hacked.

  • Examples: Tangem, Ledger Nano, Bitkey
  • Pros: Maximum security, good for long-term holding
  • Cons: Less convenient for quick trading

3. Custodial Wallets

These wallets are controlled by a third-party service like a crypto exchange. The provider keeps your private keys. This makes things easier, but also means you trust them with your funds.

  • Examples: Coinbase, Crypto.com
  • Pros: Easy recovery, simple for beginners
  • Cons: Less privacy, higher risk if the platform is hacked

4. Non-Custodial Wallets

Non-custodial wallets let you control your own private keys. No one else has access to your funds. These are popular among experienced users who want full ownership of their crypto.

  • Examples: Best Wallet, MetaMask, Tangem
  • Pros: Full control, more privacy, safer from exchange risks
  • Cons: You’re responsible for your keys — lose them, and you lose your crypto

Tip: Many users in Singapore use both hot and cold wallets — one for trading, one for safe storage.

Crypto Rules and Security in Singapore

Singapore has clear and strong rules for crypto use. This helps build trust and protects investors. The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) regulates the crypto space through the Payment Services Act. If you live in Singapore and use crypto wallets, it's good to understand how these rules work.

Why Regulation Matters

MAS sets standards for digital payment services. If a company wants to offer crypto services in Singapore, they need to get a license. This means the company follows strict rules about customer funds, anti-money laundering (AML), and security.

Using wallets or platforms that are MAS-licensed adds an extra layer of safety. For example, Coinbase Singapore and Crypto.com have local approvals. These platforms offer custodial services, but they also follow Singapore law and help users stay compliant.

KYC and Identity Checks

Most crypto platforms in Singapore require KYC (Know Your Customer) checks. This means you must verify your identity with documents. While this adds a step, it also prevents fraud and makes the system safer.

Wallet Security Features

When choosing a crypto wallet in SG, always look for strong security tools. These include:

  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds a second layer of login protection.
  • Biometric Login: Uses your fingerprint or face to unlock your wallet.
  • Multisig: Requires approval from more than one device or person.
  • Seed Phrase Backups: Lets you recover your wallet if you lose your phone.

Stay Safe from Scams

Even with strong laws, scams still happen. Be careful with links sent by unknown people, fake apps, and phishing emails. Always download wallets from official sources.

Quick note: If a wallet offers too many features without asking for ID or has zero reviews, that’s a red flag. Trusted platforms like Best Wallet and Tangem provide a good balance between privacy and protection.

Best Crypto Wallets for Singapore Users

1. Best Wallet

Best Wallet is a modern mobile wallet designed for both beginners and active crypto users. It’s especially popular in Singapore because it supports buying and selling crypto directly in SGD (Singapore dollars). You can use Apple Pay, Google Pay, credit cards, or even PayPal — all without complicated steps.

What makes Best Wallet different is its focus on privacy. You don’t need to complete KYC (identity verification) to use it. That’s great for users who prefer more control. It’s a non-custodial wallet, meaning you hold your own keys.

Security is strong. It uses 2FA, biometric login, and backup recovery phrases. The app is clean, simple, and perfect for mobile users.

Key features:

  • Buy and sell crypto with SGD
  • No KYC needed
  • Biometric and 2FA security
  • Staking and DeFi tools included
  • Launchpad support for new tokens

This wallet supports over 60 networks and tokens. Whether you’re trading Bitcoin or exploring DeFi, it’s a powerful all-in-one option. Many users pair it with a cold wallet like Tangem for extra protection.

If you want a private and flexible wallet for everyday use in Singapore, Best Wallet is one of the top picks.

2. MetaMask

MetaMask is one of the most popular crypto wallets in the world. It works as a browser extension and a mobile app. It’s mostly used for Ethereum and ERC-20 tokens. If you're using DeFi platforms, NFTs, or Web3 tools, MetaMask is almost a must-have.

In Singapore, many users connect MetaMask to platforms like Uniswap, OpenSea, and decentralized exchanges. The wallet gives full control — you hold your private keys, not the company.

Key features:

  • Non-custodial: You own the keys
  • Connects easily to Web3 apps
  • Supports NFT and token swaps
  • Open-source and widely supported

However, MetaMask doesn’t support direct fiat purchases in SGD. You need to buy crypto on another platform, like Crypto.com, and then transfer it to MetaMask. Also, since it's a hot wallet, it’s more exposed to internet threats. Be sure to enable password protection and backup your seed phrase safely.

MetaMask is best for users in Singapore who want full access to the Ethereum ecosystem and DeFi tools.

3. Tangem Wallet

Tangem Wallet is a next-generation cold wallet designed like a credit card. It uses NFC (near-field communication) and doesn’t need batteries or cables. Just tap it to your phone and manage your assets through the Tangem app.

This wallet is especially popular for long-term holders in Singapore. It stores private keys inside a secure chip (EAL 6+ certified), keeping them safe from internet attacks and hackers.

Key features:

  • Cold wallet with no internet access
  • Easy mobile tap-and-use design
  • Supports 14,000+ tokens on 85+ blockchains
  • Backup via multiple cards
  • No account or KYC required

Even though it’s a cold wallet, it works fast through the app. You can send, receive, and check balances in seconds. The wallet is ideal for pairing with hot wallets like MetaMask or Best Wallet.

If you value offline security but want something simple, Tangem is a smart pick for Singapore crypto users.

4. Bitkey

Bitkey is a Bitcoin-only wallet by Block, Inc. It focuses on long-term security and ownership. This hardware wallet uses multi-signature technology, meaning two of three devices must agree before any transaction happens (for example: your phone + hardware key).

It’s a good choice for Bitcoin investors who don’t want to depend on exchanges. You don’t need KYC, and the wallet works in over 90 countries — including Singapore.

Key features:

  • Multi-sig protection
  • Works with mobile app
  • Supports self-custody and recovery options
  • Fully focused on Bitcoin

Bitkey is not for trading altcoins or using DeFi. But for BTC holders in Singapore who want strong protection and full control, it's a solid hardware wallet option.

Simple, secure, and Bitcoin-focused — Bitkey is built for serious holders.

5. Ledger Nano (X and S Plus)

Ledger is a global leader in hardware wallets. The Nano X and S Plus models are both popular in Singapore. They support over 5,000 tokens across major blockchains including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and more.

Ledger wallets store your private keys offline and connect to your phone or computer via USB or Bluetooth (Nano X only). You manage everything through the Ledger Live app, which includes staking, portfolio tracking, and token swaps.

Key features:

  • Cold storage with strong encryption
  • Bluetooth (Nano X) or USB (S Plus)
  • Works with Ledger Live for full control
  • Supports NFTs, DeFi, and staking

Ledger is ideal for users who want high-level security and multi-chain support — all in a compact device.
