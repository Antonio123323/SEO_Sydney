Don’t Waste $100 — Do This With Crypto Instead

24/10/2025

Cryptocurrency is no longer just for tech experts or millionaires. Today, anyone can get involved — even with a small budget. $100 is all you need to start learning how the crypto world works and to make your first smart move.

But here’s the challenge: with so many coins, platforms, and opinions online, it’s easy to feel lost or make a bad choice. That’s why many new investors hesitate — or worse, jump in blindly and regret it later.

This article will guide you step-by-step through the best way to invest $100 in crypto. You’ll discover how to make safe decisions, which assets to focus on, and how to avoid the traps that cause beginners to lose money.

We’ll also introduce tools and strategies that help reduce stress and boost your confidence — including modern platforms that take care of the hard work for you.

If you want to get into crypto, learn as you go, and protect your money — this is the right place to begin.

Legal and Tax Environment for Crypto Investing

Before putting any money into crypto, you need to know what’s allowed — and what could cost you later. If you live in Switerland or any other EU country, you’re in luck: crypto is legal, recognized, and increasingly well-regulated.

Crypto legality in Switerland

Switerlandn residents can legally buy, own, and sell cryptocurrencies. These digital assets are treated as virtual property, not currency. This means you can use licensed platforms without fear of legal trouble — as long as they follow European rules.

MiCA — the new EU-wide regulation

In late 2024, the European Union rolled out MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets). This new law makes crypto safer for individuals by requiring platforms to be licensed, secure, and fully transparent.

Under MiCA, approved crypto services must:

  • Register and operate legally in the EU
  • Protect user funds and personal data
  • Offer clear details on fees, risks, and how assets are handled

This makes it easier for you to trust the platforms you’re using — as long as they are EU-compliant.

Switerlandn crypto tax: what’s changing?

Right now, Switerland does not apply tax on crypto if you simply hold coins or swap one crypto for another. But this is set to change.

From January 2026, Switerland will introduce a flat 25% tax on profits when crypto is converted into euros or used to make purchases. Holding or crypto-to-crypto trades will remain untaxed.

Action Tax Now Tax After 2026
Buy and hold crypto No tax No tax
Swap BTC for ETH No tax No tax
Sell crypto for EUR No tax 25% tax on gain
Pay for services with crypto No tax 25% tax on gain

Takeaway: Investing before 2026 gives you time to grow your portfolio without immediate tax concerns. If you hold your assets or trade within crypto, you stay in the safe zone.

How to Choose a Reliable Platform for Investing

Not all crypto platforms are created equal. If you're investing $100, you want to make sure every cent is going to the right place — not into hidden fees or unsafe services.

What to check before you sign up

Before creating an account anywhere, take a moment to review these essential points:

  • Regulation: Is the platform licensed in the EU or locally in Switerland? Regulated services are safer and follow strict rules.
  • Euro support: Can you deposit and withdraw using euros through SEPA or bank card? This makes transactions smooth and affordable.
  • Fees: Are deposit, trading, and withdrawal fees clearly stated? Transparency matters.
  • Interface: Is the dashboard clean and beginner-friendly? You should feel comfortable from day one.
  • Security: Does it offer two-factor authentication (2FA), data encryption, and trusted payment methods?

Examples of beginner-friendly platforms

  • Bitpanda: Based in Austria and regulated in the EU, this platform is perfect for new investors and supports both crypto and other digital assets.
  • Binance (Lite mode): Offers access to a wide range of cryptocurrencies with low fees and easy euro deposits. The “Lite” interface simplifies the experience.
  • Swissquote: A banking-grade platform for those who want maximum security and long-term storage solutions.

Which Cryptocurrencies Are Best to Start With

With thousands of coins on the market, it’s tempting to chase the newest or fastest-growing tokens. But when starting with just $100, it’s smarter to stick with proven, reliable cryptocurrencies.

Start with Bitcoin

Bitcoin (BTC) is the most recognized and widely used cryptocurrency. It has the longest history, highest market cap, and strongest global support.

  • Used as a store of value (“digital gold”)
  • Accepted on all major platforms
  • Lower risk compared to smaller coins

Suggested allocation: Around 60–70% of your $100.

Why Ethereum is a smart second choice

Ethereum (ETH) is the foundation of most decentralized apps and smart contracts. It powers projects in NFTs, DeFi, and more.

  • Large ecosystem with strong developer support
  • Frequently updated and improved
  • Second only to Bitcoin in market stability

Suggested allocation: 20–30% of your budget.

Optional: Add small exposure to other assets

Once you understand how crypto works, you might explore a few other coins. But keep exposure low at the start — under 10% total.

  • MATIC (Polygon): Helps reduce Ethereum transaction costs
  • USDT or USDC: Stablecoins tied to the US dollar

Example portfolio for $100:

  • $65 in Bitcoin
  • $25 in Ethereum
  • $10 in MATIC or a stablecoin

By focusing on stability and long-term value, this type of portfolio helps reduce stress and gives you a reliable foundation to build on.

Investment Strategies to Avoid Regret

It’s not just what you buy — it’s how you manage your investment that determines whether you succeed or regret the decision. A smart strategy helps you stay in control, even when prices rise or fall unexpectedly.

Use gradual investing to manage risk

One of the safest ways to invest in crypto is through a method called Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA). This means spreading your investment over several days or weeks instead of buying everything at once.

Example: Split your $100 into four parts. Buy $25 of crypto each week for a month. This helps balance the price you pay and reduces the impact of sudden market dips.

Hold instead of trading

Trading might sound exciting, but it’s risky — especially for beginners. Markets move fast, and guessing the right moment to sell or buy can lead to panic decisions.

It’s often better to buy strong coins like Bitcoin and Ethereum and hold them for several months or longer. This strategy avoids emotional stress and gives your assets time to grow.

Set clear personal goals

Are you investing to learn, to save, or to test the waters? When you have a goal, you’re less likely to make random decisions. Stick to your plan and check your progress once a month — not every hour.

Take security seriously

Even with just $100, you should protect your investment:

  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your crypto platform
  • Use strong, unique passwords — never reuse passwords from other accounts
  • Keep your recovery phrases or keys in a safe, offline place

Bonus tip: Don’t fall for “get rich quick” offers or trading signals from unknown sources. Focus on long-term success instead of short-term hype.

Mistakes to Avoid When Investing $100 in Crypto

Even a small investment deserves a smart approach. Many beginners lose money not because of bad luck, but because of avoidable errors. Here are some of the most common mistakes to steer clear of when starting with $100 in crypto.

Trusting unknown platforms

Not every crypto website is safe. Some are poorly managed. Others are outright scams. If a platform offers “guaranteed profits” or demands fast deposits — that’s a red flag.

What to do: Only use platforms that are regulated in the EU or have a trusted reputation. Always read reviews and double-check legal compliance before signing up.

Going all-in on one coin

Spending your entire $100 on a single cryptocurrency — especially a trending one — is risky. Prices can rise fast, but they can crash even faster.

What to do: Diversify your small budget. Stick to solid assets like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Use stablecoins if you want to hold value without volatility.

Following hype and social media tips

Crypto Twitter and TikTok are full of "hot tips." But many of these are based on trends, not research. Following them blindly could cost you your entire investment.

What to do: Base decisions on facts — not memes or influencers. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

Ignoring fees and minimums

Some platforms charge high fees for deposits, trades, or withdrawals — especially with small balances. These fees can quietly drain your $100.

What to do: Compare platforms. Choose services with low and transparent fees. Avoid unnecessary transactions that cost more than they earn.

Reacting emotionally to price changes

Crypto moves fast. One day your balance is up 10%, the next it drops. Many people panic sell at the worst possible moment — then regret it.

What to do: Have a plan. Expect ups and downs. Don’t make decisions out of fear or excitement. Stay calm, and think long-term.

The Future of the Crypto Market: Should You Wait?

“Maybe I should wait until prices drop…” — this is one of the most common thoughts beginners have. But timing the crypto market is extremely difficult, even for professionals. The truth is: waiting can cost you more than starting small right now.

Crypto is maturing — not disappearing

In the past, crypto was viewed as a Wild West. Today, it’s becoming a regulated and growing financial system. With laws like MiCA in the EU and strong interest from global banks and companies, crypto is entering a more stable phase.

Bitcoin and Ethereum are no longer “experiments.” They’re widely accepted digital assets that are being integrated into the future of finance.

Why now is a better time than you think

Markets go up and down — that’s normal. But historically, people who invested small amounts consistently over time have done much better than those who tried to guess the perfect moment.

Example: Someone who invested $25/month into Bitcoin over 12 months — during both high and low prices — would likely see strong gains by now, compared to someone who waited a whole year to invest a full $300.

Start learning, not just earning

Investing $100 is not just about profit — it’s about learning. You’ll understand how platforms work, how to manage security, and how markets react to news. These lessons are valuable and cost very little if you start small.

Bottom line: Instead of trying to time the perfect market entry, focus on building experience. Even during sideways or slow markets, your knowledge compounds — just like your assets.
