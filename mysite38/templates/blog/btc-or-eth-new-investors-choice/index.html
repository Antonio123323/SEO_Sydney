BTC or ETH? Here’s What New Investors Are Choosing

25/10/2025

Cryptocurrencies have surged from niche curiosities to mainstream financial assets, attracting everyday individuals seeking growth and autonomy. With so many options and conflicting opinions, newcomers often feel unsure where to begin.

This guide simplifies the journey. Crafted for first-time investors, it offers clear, actionable steps. You’ll discover how to:

  • Understand what sets crypto markets apart
  • Define your financial objectives and risk boundaries
  • Select solid coins with genuine use cases
  • Secure your investments using best practices

This isn’t about overnight wealth—it’s about steady, informed progress. Whether you invest €250 or more, this roadmap helps you move forward with confidence and purpose.

Understanding the Crypto Market

Before investing a single euro, it's crucial to know how the crypto space behaves. It operates 24/7, reacts swiftly to global news, and swings more dramatically than traditional assets.

  • Decentralized digital assets: These operate on blockchain networks, free from bank control.
  • Always open: Crypto exchanges trade non-stop—even on weekends and holidays.
  • High volatility: Daily fluctuations of 10–30% are common, which can be both opportunity and danger.

Recognize that crypto is swift, emotionally driven, and requires careful navigation.

Define Your Investment Goals

Investing without clarity leads to impulsive decisions. To be effective, you need to outline your intentions and understand your risk comfort level.

  • Long-term growth: Accumulate strong coins and hold for several years
  • Passive income: Generate earnings through staking or yield-based platforms
  • Short-term gains: Trade market swings—requires skill and carries more risk

Assess how you'd cope with significant price drops and whether your funds are genuinely flexible.

  • Keep crypto exposure to a conservative 5–10% of your total portfolio
  • Diversify within crypto across a few strong assets rather than many speculative ones

How to Evaluate a Cryptocurrency Project

Not every token deserves your attention. Here's a checklist to determine if a project has substance:

  • Purpose: Does it solve a real problem?
  • Tokenomics: Is supply capped? Does it offer benefits to holders?
  • Team: Are the founders experienced and transparent?
  • Development: Is code actively maintained and audited?
  • Community: Is there real user engagement?

Doing this homework can protect you from hype-driven projects with no long-term value.

Top Cryptocurrencies better for beginners

Start with established digital assets that offer reliability and broad adoption:

  • Bitcoin (BTC): The original digital store of value with limited supply.
  • Ethereum (ETH): Powers smart contracts, DeFi, and NFTs; now more sustainable post-upgrade.
  • Other major coins: Polygon, Solana, Cardano—consider them once you’re comfortable.
  • Stablecoins: USDT, USDC, DAI—used for preserving value and earning yield.

Avoid jumping into meme coins or unverified tokens with no real purpose.

Choosing a Reliable Crypto Exchange

Selecting an exchange is a critical first step. Here’s what to look for:

  • Regulatory compliance: Prefer EU-regulated platforms under MiCA.
  • Security protections: Features like two-factor authentication and offline storage.
  • Euro support: Choose exchanges with SEPA transfers and card deposits.
  • Transparent costs: Look for clear trading, withdrawal, and deposit fees.
  • User-friendly tools: Intuitive interfaces and multilingual support make a difference.

Start with a trusted centralized exchange before exploring decentralized alternatives.

Wallets and Safe Storage

Buying crypto is just the beginning—protecting it is just as crucial. Here are smart storage practices:

  • Custodial wallets: Managed by exchanges—easy but rely on third-party security.
  • Non-custodial wallets: You hold the keys—greater control and responsibility.
  • Hot vs. cold storage: Hot wallets are convenient online; cold wallets (hardware) are safest.

Stay secure by:

  • Backing up recovery phrases offline
  • Enabling 2FA wherever possible
  • Avoiding phishing by using official bookmarks

Investment Strategies for Beginners

How you invest is key to your success. Here are proven approaches:

  • Dollar-cost averaging: Invest the same amount periodically to reduce timing risk.
  • HODLing: Buy and hold quality assets through market cycles.
  • Wait on trading: Avoid day-trading until you're experienced.
  • Rule-setting: Use stop-losses, track trades, and set target exits.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Watch out for these traps:

  • Hype-based investments: Always do thorough homework.
  • Overexposure: Never invest more than you can afford to lose.
  • Neglecting security: Protect your keys and accounts vigilantly.
  • No strategy: Trade with purpose, not impulse.
  • Trusting too-good-to-be-true offers: High guaranteed returns are often scams.

Regulatory Considerations in the EU/Switerland

Legal clarity is improving—but remains vital for safe crypto investing:

  • MiCA mandates transparency and accountability for EU platforms
  • Switerland permits crypto gains but taxes trades and staking income
  • Use compliant platforms, keep accurate transaction records, and monitor tax guidance

Conclusion

Smart crypto investing is built on clarity, discipline, and secure foundations. Define your goals, research each asset, protect your funds, and follow a proven strategy—you don’t need to be an expert to succeed.

Take informed steps, prioritize security, and let time with good strategy do the work. Your journey starts here. Happy investing!
