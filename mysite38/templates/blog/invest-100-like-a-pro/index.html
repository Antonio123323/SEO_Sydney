Legal and Tax Environment for Crypto Investing

Before making your first crypto purchase, it’s important to understand the rules and tax laws that apply in your country. In places like Switerland and the wider EU, the legal climate for digital currencies has become much clearer in recent years.

Is cryptocurrency legal in Switerland?

Yes. You’re allowed to buy, hold, and trade crypto assets in Switerland. These assets are considered virtual property, not official currency, which means you can use crypto platforms legally — as long as they follow EU rules.

New EU rules: MiCA regulation

The European Union has introduced a framework called MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets), designed to protect users and make the crypto market safer. It applies to all EU countries, including Switerland.

Under MiCA, trading platforms must:

  • Be officially registered or licensed in the EU
  • Keep user funds secure and separate
  • Clearly explain risks and fees

That’s why it’s smart to use EU-compliant platforms for your crypto journey — like TokenTact, which follows local regulations and makes onboarding simple.

How crypto taxes work in Switerland

Currently, if you buy crypto and just hold it or swap it for another coin, there’s no tax. But a new system is coming.

Starting in 2026, if you sell your crypto for euros or use it to buy goods, Switerland will apply a flat 25% tax on profits. Crypto-to-crypto exchanges will still be tax-free.

Note: If you begin investing before 2026, you may benefit from this tax-free window — especially if you hold or make crypto-to-crypto swaps only.

How to Choose a Reliable Platform for Investing

Choosing where to invest is just as important as choosing what to invest in. If you're starting with $100, every euro counts — so the platform you pick must be secure, regulated, and easy to use.

What makes a good crypto platform?

There are hundreds of platforms out there, but not all of them are trustworthy. Here’s what you should look for before signing up:

  • Official registration: Make sure the platform is licensed in the EU or follows EU crypto rules (like MiCA).
  • Supports euro payments: Look for SEPA transfers, bank cards, or Apple/Google Pay for fast deposits and withdrawals.
  • Low and transparent fees: Avoid platforms with hidden costs or expensive withdrawal charges.
  • Simple interface: You should be able to buy and track your crypto with just a few clicks.
  • Good security: Two-factor login (2FA), email confirmations, and fund protection are essential.

Which Cryptocurrencies Are Best to Start With

When investing $100 in crypto, the biggest mistake is picking the wrong coins. Some tokens are trendy but risky, while others are stable and trusted. As a beginner, your goal should be simple: protect your money and learn safely.

Why Bitcoin is a smart first choice

Bitcoin (BTC) is the original cryptocurrency and still the most valuable. It’s often called “digital gold” because people use it to store value over time. It has the biggest user base, the highest security, and strong support across all platforms.

Start by putting 60–70% of your $100 into BTC. It’s a strong foundation that helps balance your portfolio and reduce risk.

Why Ethereum belongs in your portfolio

Ethereum (ETH) is the second-largest crypto. It powers smart contracts and most Web3 projects, including NFTs and decentralized apps. If you want exposure to real blockchain use cases, ETH is the way to go.

Consider allocating 20–30% of your investment to Ethereum.

What about altcoins and stablecoins?

Once you’re comfortable with BTC and ETH, you can explore other coins. But for your first $100, only a small portion should go to lesser-known assets.

  • Altcoins: Like MATIC (Polygon) or LINK (Chainlink). Use with caution — no more than 10% of your total.
  • Stablecoins: Like USDT or USDC, backed by fiat currency. Good if you want to wait or pause trading without going back to euros.

Example beginner portfolio

  • $65 in Bitcoin (BTC)
  • $25 in Ethereum (ETH)
  • $10 in USDT or MATIC (optional)

Keep it simple at first. Your goal is not to gamble — it’s to build knowledge, avoid regret, and protect your small investment from major losses.

Investment Strategies to Avoid Regret

Even a small budget like $100 needs a smart strategy. Many people lose money in crypto not because they invest too little — but because they invest without a plan. Below are easy and safe ways to protect your funds and avoid mistakes.

Use the “slow and steady” method

The safest way to invest in crypto is through Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA). Instead of putting in all your money at once, you invest a small part each week or month.

Example: Break your $100 into 4 parts and buy $25 of Bitcoin or Ethereum every week. This way, you won’t panic if the price drops — you’ll just get more value next time.

Avoid trading — focus on holding

New users often try to “buy low and sell high” — but crypto is very volatile. Prices move fast, and emotional decisions can lead to losses.

The better choice for beginners is to buy and hold (HODL). Bitcoin and Ethereum have grown in value over time. Holding for months or years gives you a higher chance of profit, without stress.

Set clear goals before investing

Ask yourself: Why are you doing this? To learn? To save long-term? To test how crypto works? Knowing your reason helps you stay focused and avoid emotional reactions when prices move up or down.

Keep your account secure

No matter how much you invest, your account must be protected. Here are three key rules:

  • Turn on 2FA (two-factor authentication) — it blocks unauthorized logins.
  • Use a strong, unique password — never reuse one from another site.
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi when checking your crypto wallet or platform.

Once your portfolio grows, you might consider moving assets to a personal wallet. But for small investments, trusted platforms like TokenTact are safe and convenient to begin with.

Mistakes to Avoid When Investing $100 in Crypto

Investing in crypto doesn’t require a big budget, but it does require smart choices. Many beginners lose their $100 because they make avoidable errors. Here are the top mistakes to watch out for — and how to avoid them.

Using random or unknown platforms

Never trust a platform just because it shows up in ads or social media. Scams are everywhere. If a website looks suspicious, or promises unrealistic profits, stay away.

Solution: Use only verified, regulated platforms. Choose services that follow EU law and support safe payment options like SEPA or bank cards.

Putting everything into one coin

It’s risky to invest your full $100 into a single cryptocurrency — especially if it’s new or highly volatile. Even Bitcoin can go through sharp dips.

Diversify your portfolio. A mix of Bitcoin, Ethereum, and a stablecoin helps lower your risk and smooth out price changes.

Chasing hype coins or viral trends

Social media is full of “the next big coin” stories. But most of these tokens rise fast and crash even faster. You could lose everything overnight.

Tip: Stick with top assets. If you want to explore small altcoins, limit your exposure to 5–10% of your budget.

Ignoring transaction fees

Fees might seem small, but they add up — especially with a $100 budget. Always check deposit, withdrawal, and trading fees before confirming a transaction.

Use platforms that show fees clearly and support low-cost euro deposits. Automated solutions like TokenTact help reduce unnecessary costs for new users.

Reacting emotionally

Crypto markets move fast. Prices can jump or crash in minutes. New investors often panic when the price drops and sell at a loss — or buy high from fear of missing out (FOMO).

Stick to your plan, don’t check prices every hour, and avoid reacting to every swing. Consistency wins in the long run.
