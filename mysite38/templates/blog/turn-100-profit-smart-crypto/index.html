Let’s get started.

Legal and Tax Environment for Crypto Investing

Before investing any money in crypto, it's important to understand the legal and tax situation in your country. If you live in the European Union or Switerland, good news — cryptocurrency is legal and regulated.

Is crypto legal in Switerland?

Yes. In Switerland, cryptocurrencies are treated as virtual assets. You can buy, sell, and hold crypto without legal problems. The government allows crypto trading, and many people already use it as an investment option.

New rules across Europe (MiCA)

Since late 2024, the European Union has introduced a regulation called Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA). This law makes crypto safer for users. It forces platforms to follow strict rules — like keeping user funds safe, preventing fraud, and being transparent about fees.

  • Platforms must be licensed in the EU
  • Stablecoins are more regulated to avoid risks
  • Investor protection is stronger than before

If you use a regulated trading platform, you’re already on the right path.

Crypto taxes in Switerland

Right now, Switerland has friendly tax rules for crypto. If you buy and hold coins, and later swap them for other cryptocurrencies, you don’t pay any taxes. But this is going to change.

Starting in 2026, a new law will introduce a flat 25% tax on crypto profits — but only when you convert crypto into euros or use it to pay for goods. If you just hold or trade between coins, there is still no tax.

Here’s a simple overview:

Action Tax before 2026 Tax after 2026
Hold BTC or ETH No tax No tax
Swap BTC to ETH No tax No tax
Sell crypto for EUR No tax 25% tax on profit
Use crypto to buy a product No tax 25% tax on profit

Tip: If you're starting now, you can still enjoy the no-tax period before 202 That’s a good reason to begin small and learn how the market works.

How to Choose a Reliable Platform for Investing

When investing $100 in crypto, choosing the right platform is just as important as choosing the right coin. A good platform makes investing simple, safe, and affordable. A bad one can lead to lost money or wasted time.

What to look for in a crypto platform

Here are the most important things to check before signing up:

  • License and regulation: Always choose a platform that is licensed in the EU or Switerland. This means it follows strict rules to protect your money.
  • Euro support: Make sure the platform allows you to deposit and withdraw in euros using SEPA bank transfer or card.
  • Low fees: High trading or withdrawal fees can eat up a large part of your $100. Look for platforms with clear, fair pricing.
  • Easy interface: As a beginner, you need a simple dashboard with clear instructions.
  • Security features: Choose platforms that use 2FA (two-factor authentication), cold storage, and encryption to protect your funds.

Top platforms for crypto investing in Switerland and the EU

Here are three platforms that are trusted, regulated, and beginner-friendly:

  • Binance: One of the largest platforms in the world. Supports SEPA transfers, offers hundreds of coins, and has low fees. Interface can be complex for beginners, but there is a “Lite” version.
  • Bitpanda: Based in Austria, fully regulated in the EU. Very beginner-friendly. Supports crypto, stocks, and metals. Slightly higher fees but great for small investors.
  • Swissquote: A Swiss bank with crypto services. High trust level, strong security. Best for people who want long-term holding with bank-level service.

All of these platforms support euro deposits, offer a good choice of cryptocurrencies, and follow EU law. But they still require manual actions — like choosing coins and placing orders. For beginners who want less stress, automated options can be a better fit (more on this in section 8).

Which Cryptocurrencies Are Best to Start With

With hundreds of coins on the market, choosing where to put your $100 can be confusing. But don’t worry. You don’t need to chase every new trend. The smartest way to start is with strong, proven cryptocurrencies.

Start with Bitcoin (BTC)

Bitcoin is the first and most well-known cryptocurrency. It has been around since 2009 and is considered the “digital gold” of the crypto world. If you want to invest safely, putting 60–70% of your $100 into Bitcoin is a solid choice.

  • Why choose Bitcoin?
  • It’s the most trusted crypto asset
  • It has strong long-term growth history
  • It’s accepted on all major platforms

Add Ethereum (ETH) to your mix

Ethereum is the second-biggest cryptocurrency. It supports smart contracts and decentralized apps (dApps). Many new crypto projects are built on Ethereum. You can safely put 20–30% of your $100 into ETH.

  • Used for NFTs, DeFi, and Web3 projects
  • Large developer community
  • Regular upgrades to improve performance

Should you buy other coins?

If you're just starting, it’s best to avoid smaller or new coins. These can offer big gains, but also big losses. Once you understand the market, you can explore altcoins like:

  • Polygon (MATIC): Low-cost transactions on Ethereum
  • Chainlink (LINK): A key player in connecting crypto to real-world data

But limit these to no more than 10% of your total budget. Focus on safety first.

What about stablecoins?

Stablecoins like USDT or USDC are digital currencies linked to real currencies like the dollar. They’re useful if you want to hold value without volatility. You can keep a small portion of your investment in stablecoins while you watch the market.

Example Portfolio for $100:

  • $65 in Bitcoin (BTC)
  • $25 in Ethereum (ETH)
  • $10 in a stablecoin (USDT) or altcoin like MATIC

This mix gives you exposure to strong assets while staying low-risk.

Investment Strategies to Avoid Regret

Even with just $100, a smart plan can make a big difference. The goal is not to get rich overnight, but to avoid losses and build confidence. Here are a few simple strategies to help you invest with less risk.

Use the DCA method

Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) means investing small amounts over time instead of all at once. For example, instead of spending all $100 today, you could invest $25 every week for a month.

  • It reduces the risk of buying at the wrong time
  • You learn how the market behaves
  • It builds discipline and patience

Example: Buy $25 of BTC this week. If the price drops, next week’s $25 buys more. Over time, your average cost is balanced.

Hold, don’t trade

New investors often try to trade coins quickly, hoping to make fast profits. But this is risky and stressful. Unless you have experience, it’s better to buy and hold (HODL).

Many successful investors simply hold Bitcoin and Ethereum for months or years. History shows that long-term holders usually win — especially during market growth cycles.

Set clear goals

Ask yourself: Why are you investing? Is it to learn, to grow money, or to prepare for the future? Having a clear goal helps you stay focused and avoid panic during price drops.

Protect your investment

Even a small investment needs protection. Use these security steps:

  • Enable 2FA (two-factor authentication) on your trading platform
  • Use a strong password and store it safely
  • Don’t share account details with others

If you plan to hold for the long term, consider using a personal crypto wallet instead of leaving everything on the exchange.

Mistakes to Avoid When Investing $100 in Crypto

Investing in crypto can be exciting, but it’s easy to make mistakes—especially when starting with a small amount like $100. Avoiding these common errors will help you protect your money and reduce stress.

Using untrusted platforms

Many new users sign up for platforms they see in ads or social media. Some of these platforms are not licensed or secure. If you send money to the wrong place, you may never get it back.

Always check:

  • Is the platform regulated in the EU?
  • Does it support euro deposits like SEPA?
  • Does it have clear information about fees and security?

Putting all your money into one coin

It may feel smart to go “all-in” on one coin that’s rising. But that’s risky. Even strong coins like Bitcoin and Ethereum can drop in price.

Diversify your small investment. A mix of BTC, ETH, and maybe one altcoin or stablecoin can help reduce risk.

Chasing hype and meme coins

Many new investors are tempted by fast-rising tokens like Dogecoin or others promoted on social media. These coins often grow fast—but crash even faster.

Example: A coin may go up 200% in one week, but lose it all the next. With only $100 to invest, there’s no room for that kind of risk.

Ignoring fees

Fees matter. A $3 fee on a $10 trade is a 30% loss. Always check:

  • Deposit fees
  • Trading fees
  • Withdrawal fees

Choose platforms with low fees and no hidden costs.

Letting emotions lead

Fear, greed, and hype can lead to bad choices. Don’t panic-sell when prices drop. Don’t jump in because of social media trends. Stick to your plan, and invest calmly.

The Future of the Crypto Market: Should You Wait?

Many people ask, “Is now the right time to invest in crypto?” The truth is, no one can predict the perfect time. But you don’t need perfect timing to start with $100.

Crypto is growing — globally and in the EU

Bitcoin and Ethereum have survived many market cycles. Each time, they come back stronger. New technologies, better platforms, and clearer laws are making crypto more stable and trusted.

In the EU, new rules like MiCA are helping build a safer market. Big banks and governments are now joining the crypto space. This makes it more likely that prices will grow long-term.

Should you wait for the price to drop?

You can wait for a “dip,” but markets don’t move in straight lines. Prices go up and down every day. The best strategy is to start small and invest regularly—like $20 each month. That way, you don’t risk all your money at once.

Example:

  • If you had invested $100 in Bitcoin in July 2022, you would have more than doubled your money by mid-202
  • But even those who bought during a peak and held long-term usually recover and profit later.

Don’t wait to learn

The earlier you start, the more experience you gain. Even a small investment helps you understand how platforms work, how prices move, and what news affects the market.

Conclusion: You don’t need to wait for the “perfect time.” You need to start learning and building confidence. And $100 is a safe, smart way to begin.
