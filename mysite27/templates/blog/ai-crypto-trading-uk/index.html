Costs, Slippage & ROI Math: The Economics of Automation

Every trading strategy, whether manual or automated, involves costs. In ai in crypto trading uk, understanding these costs helps traders evaluate real profitability. AI systems may look efficient on paper, but factors such as fees, slippage, and infrastructure expenses can significantly impact returns.

Trading fees are the most visible cost. Exchanges charge for both maker and taker orders, and when bots execute dozens or hundreds of trades per day, these fees quickly add up. Even a 0.1% transaction cost can reduce long-term gains if not managed properly.

Slippage — the difference between the expected and actual trade price — is another silent expense. It often happens when markets move fast or liquidity is low. For UK traders using crypto automation bots uk, reducing slippage through smarter order routing and execution timing is a key performance goal.

There are also indirect costs to consider:

  • Cloud servers or VPS hosting for round-the-clock operation.
  • Paid market data or premium API subscriptions.
  • AI model training resources and software licences.
  • Compliance tools and tax reporting services.

Calculating the return on investment (ROI) involves subtracting all these costs from gross profits. A simple formula used by UK traders is:

Net ROI = (Total Gains – Total Costs) ÷ Total Costs × 100%

Platforms like Immediate Connect (https://immediateconnect-gb.com/) help traders visualise these economics in real time. Dashboards show fee impact, trade efficiency, and slippage averages. This lets users evaluate trading bots performance uk based on true net returns, not just gross profit.
