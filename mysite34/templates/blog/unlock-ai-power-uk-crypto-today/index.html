AI in Anti-Fraud, AML and Compliance

Fraud and money laundering are serious problems in the crypto space. Because blockchain is anonymous and global, bad actors often use it to hide illegal activity. That’s why companies and regulators in the UK are now turning to AI-driven tools to help detect and stop suspicious behaviour.

Elliptic: AI for blockchain investigations

One of the UK’s most well-known firms in this field is Elliptic, based in London. They use AI to scan blockchain transactions and look for signs of fraud, scams, and money laundering. Their systems analyze millions of transactions across Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other networks. If a wallet or address shows suspicious patterns, it is flagged for further investigation.

Elliptic works with major crypto exchanges, banks, and even government agencies. Their tools help businesses stay compliant with UK laws and international regulations like AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and KYC (Know Your Customer).

How AI improves fraud detection

Traditional fraud tools use fixed rules. But AI can do more. It can learn from new data and find hidden patterns. This means it can:

  • Detect unusual transaction flows in real time
  • Recognize new types of scams and attack methods
  • Score users and wallets based on risk
  • Predict fraud before it happens

For example, if a wallet suddenly sends small amounts to many addresses, AI might link that to known "dusting" attacks. Or if funds move across chains too quickly, it could suggest money laundering.

Why this matters in the UK

The UK government is taking crypto crime seriously. The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) requires platforms to monitor risks and report suspicious activities. AI helps meet these requirements faster and more accurately.

Blockchain Analytics and Smart Data Systems

Blockchain generates a huge amount of data every second. But this data can be hard to understand. That’s where artificial intelligence comes in. AI can analyze blockchain transactions and patterns quickly, helping users and businesses make smarter decisions.

On-chain analytics with AI

AI tools can track the flow of crypto assets on the blockchain in real time. They spot trends like big wallet moves, sudden spikes in trading, or unusual token activity. This helps investors detect whale movements or possible market manipulation early.

For example, AI can identify when a large holder (“whale”) moves funds to an exchange. This might signal a big sale and a possible price drop. With this information, traders can act before the market reacts.

Smart contract auditing

Smart contracts are automated programs on the blockchain that manage transactions. But they can have bugs or security flaws. AI-powered tools now help audit these contracts by scanning code and spotting errors faster than manual reviews.

This technology is growing in the UK, where many DeFi projects depend on safe smart contracts. Using AI for auditing helps avoid costly hacks and builds trust among users.

Regulatory Evolution: How UK Authorities Adapt to AI and Crypto

The UK is known for its strong financial regulations. With the rise of crypto AI and blockchain technology, regulators have new challenges to face. They need to protect consumers and markets without blocking innovation.

FCA’s role and new hires

The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) is the UK’s main regulator for crypto assets. Recently, the FCA hired experts from major banks like Standard Chartered to strengthen its crypto and payments teams. These new hires bring AI and fintech knowledge to improve oversight.

The FCA encourages firms to use AI responsibly. It wants to ensure that automated tools for crypto trading and compliance follow strict rules. This helps prevent risks like fraud, market abuse, and unfair practices.

Bank of England’s AI Consortium

The Bank of England launched an AI Consortium to study how AI can improve financial services. This group brings together banks, fintechs, regulators, and researchers. It focuses on the benefits and risks of AI in areas like crypto trading and blockchain analysis.

This initiative helps the UK stay ahead in the global AI race. It also supports creating clear guidelines for crypto firms using AI technologies.

Government’s AI Opportunities Action Plan

In early 2025, the UK government published the AI Opportunities Action Plan. This plan includes 50 recommendations to boost AI development across industries, including fintech and crypto. It aims to balance innovation with safety and ethics.

Industry calls for clearer blockchain policy

The crypto industry wants similar support for blockchain as it gets for AI. Leaders have asked the government to appoint a blockchain ambassador and create a forum for dialogue between businesses and regulators. This would help align policies for AI, quantum tech, and digital assets.

Warnings from experts

Former UK Chancellor George Osborne has warned that Britain risks falling behind other countries like the US and Singapore. He stresses the need for clear crypto regulations to attract investment and innovation.

All these efforts show that UK regulators are working hard to create a safe, innovative space for AI and crypto to grow together.
